Understanding the Concepts

Categorical and Continuous Variables:

In statistics, categorical variables represent data that can be divided into distinct groups or categories. Examples include gender, ethnicity, or survey responses. On the other hand, continuous variables represent data that can take any numeric value within a range. Examples include height, weight, or temperature.

Sample Size:

The sample size refers to the number of observations or participants in a study. It is important in statistical analysis as it affects the power and reliability of the results. Generally, a larger sample size provides more accurate and reliable estimates.

Purpose:

The purpose of your analysis determines the appropriate statistical test. If you want to compare the means of two groups, you may choose a test for testing the difference. If you want to examine the relationship between two variables, you may choose a test for relationship analysis. If you want to make predictions or create a model, you may choose a test for predictive analysis.

Common Test Types:

  • T-Test: Used to compare the means of two groups.
  • ANOVA: Used to compare the means of three or more groups.
  • Pearson Correlation: Used to measure the linear relationship between two continuous variables.
  • Chi-Square Test: Used to examine the relationship between two categorical variables.
  • Regression Analysis: Used to model and predict the relationship between variables.
